Hello, my name is Levent. I'm a Turkish-born mechanical engineer. I grew up in Virginia, USA for a while (go Hoos), which is how I ended up a native English speaker. I actually struggled a bit to learn Turkish, especially given how much my parents wanted to keep English strong at home. Later on I lived in Italy and went to an international school there. So overall I turned into a pretty multicultural person without strong ties to any one place.

When I'm not doing nerdy engineering stuff, I do art: 3D modeling, painting, drawing, industrial design. I'm still not great at any of it, honestly. But I think artistic expression is a core part of the soul, part of what makes us human, the same way engineering comes from a drive to solve problems. Good design is a virtue to me. An object should be made in a way that makes you want to use it, whether it's a pair of scissors or a cutting edge aircraft. Bad design in the human interface can make even the most capable object basically unusable.

Fluid mechanics really gets me because it's an invisible part of the world for most people. Getting to look into a world of swirling, diffusing, chaotic motion, the same motion that quietly runs the climate and aircraft and the sky, is honestly kind of crazy to think about. The complexity is what pulls me in, I want to understand every part of it. I also believe we owe it to the next generations to protect the environment and leave them a livable future.

I believe in the open source ethos to my core. Software and knowledge should be shared freely, available to everyone, and maintained by everyone as a public good. That's essential for developing new technologies even when they're not profitable right away, 3D printing coming out of the RepRap movement is a good example of that. I want to add to that shared body of knowledge in an accessible way going forward. I feel like it's my duty to do that.
